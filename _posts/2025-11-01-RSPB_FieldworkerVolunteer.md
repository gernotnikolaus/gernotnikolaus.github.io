 In September and October, I had the amazing opportunity to work with the RSPB and live on Rathlin Island (Northern Ireland) as part of the LIFE Raft Project. This large-scale seabird conservation initiative aims to protect species such as Puffins, Guillemots, and Razorbills by managing invasive species and restoring the islandâ€™s ecosystem. Across the island, over 6,500 bait stations are monitored and managed through GIS. I worked in remote areas and rough weather conditions to collect field data, check and maintain stations, and identify remaining predators. Besides that, I strengthened my bird and plant ID skills and gained hands-on experience in practical conservation supported by geospatial technology.
